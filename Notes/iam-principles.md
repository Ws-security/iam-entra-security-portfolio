# IAM Principles

## Identity
Identity is the foundation of access control. In Microsoft Entra, identities can represent users, groups, applications, and administrative roles.

## Authentication
Authentication verifies that a user is who they claim to be.

## Authorization
Authorization determines what an authenticated user is allowed to access.

## Least Privilege
Least privilege means granting only the minimum level of access needed for a task.

## Role Separation
Administrative roles should be separated and assigned carefully to reduce risk.

## Group-Based Access
Using groups for access assignments creates a more scalable and manageable identity model than assigning permissions directly to individual users.

## Strong Authentication
Authentication methods such as Microsoft Authenticator and Temporary Access Pass help strengthen identity security.

## Identity Governance
Identity governance helps organizations control lifecycle, access reviews, and privilege management over time.
